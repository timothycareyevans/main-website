Questions we hear a lot

What is human-centered design? +
A method that starts with the people you're designing for — their behaviors, needs, and contexts — and iterates toward solutions through observation, prototyping, and testing rather than assumption.
Which industries do you work in? +
Health, mobility, retail, financial services, consumer products, education, and the public sector. The method travels; the domain expertise comes from pairing our teams with yours.
How is this different from a strategy firm? +
We prototype. Strategy here isn't a document — it's something you can touch, test with real customers, and revise before you commit capital to it.
How do engagements usually start? +
A conversation about the outcome you're after, then a scoped first phase — typically 6–12 weeks — with a working prototype or validated direction at the end.
